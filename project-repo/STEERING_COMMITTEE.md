## TSC Responsibilities

The TSC is responsible for:

- Setting the technical vision and roadmap for the project
- Making final decisions on technical matters when consensus cannot be reached
- Approving changes to governance documents and project policies
- Managing project releases and release processes
- Overseeing the security of the project and responding to security incidents
- Representing the project to the Linux Foundation and external parties
- Ensuring the long-term health, sustainability, and growth of the project
- Resolving disputes among maintainers or community members
- Approving new maintainers and TSC members
- Managing project infrastructure and resources

## TSC Membership

### Eligibility

TSC members are elected from the maintainer community. To be eligible for TSC membership, a candidate must:

- Be a current maintainer of the project in good standing
- Have demonstrated sustained, high-quality contributions to the project
- Have shown leadership and commitment to the project's goals and community
- Be willing and able to commit time to TSC responsibilities

### Election Process

1. **Nomination**: Any maintainer may nominate a candidate (including themselves) by opening an issue or pull request.

2. **Nomination period**: Nominations are open for [number] business days.

3. **Discussion**: The community discusses the nominees during the nomination period.

4. **Voting**: TSC members vote on nominees. A quorum of TSC members must participate, as defined in the [Technical Charter][charter-link].

5. **Approval**: Nominees are approved by a [majority/two-thirds majority] vote of the TSC.

6. **Announcement**: New TSC members are announced to the community and added to this file.

[charter-link]: [Link to Technical Charter]

### Term Length

TSC members serve a term of [number] years. There is no limit on the number of terms a member may serve.

Terms are staggered to ensure continuity of leadership.

### Stepping Down

TSC members may step down at any time by:

1. Notifying the TSC Chair or another TSC member
2. Opening a pull request to remove themselves from this file

We appreciate the service of all TSC members and understand that circumstances change.

### Removal

A TSC member may be removed for:

- Violation of the Code of Conduct
- Failure to fulfill TSC responsibilities
- Extended inactivity without prior arrangement
- Other conduct detrimental to the project

The removal process is:

1. A TSC member raises the concern privately with the TSC Chair (or another TSC member if the concern involves the Chair).

2. The TSC discusses the matter privately with the member in question.

3. If the issue cannot be resolved, a vote is held. Removal requires a two-thirds majority of the TSC (excluding the member in question).

4. The decision is documented and the member is removed from this file.

### Decision-Making

The TSC uses a consensus-seeking model:

1. **Proposal**: A proposal is made via the mailing list, issue, or in a meeting.

2. **Discussion**: TSC members discuss the proposal.

3. **Consensus**: If no objections remain, the proposal is adopted.

4. **Voting**: If consensus cannot be reached, a vote is called:
   - Each TSC member has one vote
   - Decisions require a [majority/two-thirds majority] of voting TSC members
   - Votes are recorded in meeting notes or the relevant issue/PR
